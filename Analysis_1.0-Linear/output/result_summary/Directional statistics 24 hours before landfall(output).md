## Key Findings

1. **Westward motion dominates**: NW + W together account for **82.74%**.
2. **Most common direction**: Northwest (NW) accounts for **57.14%** – more than half of all typhoons move northwest.
3. **No southward motion**: No typhoon has a dominant southward direction (S direction frequency = 0).
4. **Very few eastward motions**: E + NE + SE total only 15 occurrences (4.47%).
5. **Typical characteristics**: The data are consistent with the pattern of western North Pacific typhoons moving northwest after genesis, eventually making landfall or recurving.

> **Summary**: Typhoons in this dataset predominantly move northwest and west, consistent with the geographical characteristics of typhoon landfalls in China.
